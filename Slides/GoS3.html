Problem: deciding whether or not a graph is planar

How to decide if a graph $G$ is planar or not?

  • Try to draw $G$ on the plane
  • If $G$ is Hamiltonian (or nearly), can use Planarity Algorithm
  • Kuratowski's theorem: $G$ is not planar if and only if it has a subgraph $H$ that is a subdivision of $K_5$ or $K_{3,3}$.

Surfaces

A surface is a space that "locally looks like" $\mathbb{R}^2$

  • $\mathbb{R}^2$
  • Sphere $S^2$
  • Torus $T$
  • Mobius band $M$...

Drawing graphs on $\mathbb{R}^2$ same as drawing them on $S^2$. What about $T, M$?
